The New Testament

Explore the Gospels, apostolic history, letters, and prophetic revelations.

The Gospels & History
  • The account of Jesus as the promised Messianic King, written primarily to Jewish audiences.
  • A fast-paced narrative detailing Jesus as the active, suffering Servant of God.
  • An orderly, detailed historical investigation emphasizing Jesus as the Savior of all humanity.
  • A deep, theological gospel focusing on the deity of Christ and signs leading to eternal life.
  • The history of the early Church, the coming of the Holy Spirit, and the expansion of global missions.
Pauline Epistles
  • A theological defense of righteousness by faith, justification, and God's plan for Jews and Gentiles.
  • Addressing division, morality, spiritual gifts, and corporate worship standards within a chaotic local church.
  • Paul’s defense of his apostolic authority, detailing ministry hardships and the beauty of generosity.
  • A passionate defense of Christian liberty and salvation by grace alone against legalistic additions.
  • The mystery of the unified body of Christ, spiritual blessings, and the armor of spiritual warfare.
  • A warm letter from prison centered on the humility of Christ, internal joy, and absolute peace.
  • Proclaiming the absolute supremacy and sufficiency of Christ over deceptive philosophies.
  • Encouragement during persecution and foundational comfort regarding the future return of Christ.
  • Clarifying misconceptions about the Day of the Lord and warning against idleness in daily life.
  • Pastoral guidance on sound doctrine, leadership qualifications, and church administration.
  • Paul’s final charge to stay faithful, endure suffering, and preach the inspired Word of God.
  • Instructions on appointing elders and fostering healthy, godly behavior across generations in Crete.
  • A personal appeal for reconciliation, asking a master to welcome a runaway slave back as a brother.
General Epistles & Prophecy
  • An exposition showing Christ as superior to angels, Moses, the old priesthood, and legalistic sacrifices.
  • Highly practical wisdom emphasizing that genuine faith naturally demonstrates itself through action and words.
  • Comfort and ethical counsel for exiled Christians enduring institutional hostility and suffering.
  • A warning against deceptive internal teachers, paired with a confirmation of the certainty of Christ's return.
  • Reassurance of fellowship with God, defining love, truth, and providing testing criteria for real faith.
  • A short pastoral note emphasizing walking in truth and refusing hospitality to deceptive false teachers.
